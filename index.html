_about.me

I'm jexica (jessica with an x) ayran, or preferably 'xica' as in chic. I'm originally an introverted, suburban Nor-Californian from humble Mexican-Filipino roots with a million dreams, interests, and passions fueling me towards a successful New York story.

I moved to New York in 2018, after a period of endings; I finally moved out from home, my (single) mother's hip surgery was a success and I was present (all 6 months), and I also ended* my BArch studies from California College of the Arts in 2016 (*for the most part).

I, young, wild, and free of 24 years old decided to move to my favorite "American" city, the NYC. My experience here was as intense and refreshing as the seasons; pushing me to question everything: identity, name, self-love, race, values, finances, relationships (platonic and romantic), mental and physical health, spirituality, and the social constructs. I'm currently working on how to redefine it all to best fit me and give back (to my family mostly).

After meeting amazing city creative coders, a few incompatible professional experiences/industry, and a short INDIGI-tal (indigenous futurism workshop); I found myself delving into this digital realm. Later practicing with sources online, I then looked into more diverse and supportive spaces; The Knowledge House.
