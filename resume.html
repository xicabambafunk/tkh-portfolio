Student Construction Intern

Fundación Esperanza de México

Tijuana, Mexico

06/2015 - 07/2015

A few selected students had the opportunity to get flied out to Tijuana and participate in a program by a local nonprofit housing organization, Fundación Esperanza de México. They assist local families to be involved in constructing and financing their dream homes, get educated on home ownership, and receive assistance and connections with various local and international volunteers. Students are educated on actual home construction, specifically with the use of brickwork, steel-wire rebars, concrete, and reclaimed building materials (car doors, tires, etc.) practiced by Tijuana's home builders. The program also included visiting and speaking with recent deportees from the US, each with a heart-wrenching and/or inspirational tale.

  • Assembly and transportation of bricks, concrete dry and liquid mixture, and any other tools.
  • Mixing and pouring concrete floor slabs.
  • Hammering metal rebars into the correct dimensions and form.
  • Learning about Esperanza's unique hollow brick molds and brick system.
  • Connecting, cooking, and eating with the soon-to-be home-owning families.
  • Visiting the recent deportees, the border, and other architectural landmarks.

Vienna, Austra & Madrid, Barcelona

07/2015 - 08/2015

CCA’s 3-week summer studio: As "Urban Activators," students were to create a tensile-membrane multi-functional screen and stage for TEDx Madrid Salon, which was located under a bridge within a disadvantaged neighborhood in Madrid.   • Assisting in the physical assembly of the collaborative finalized design.
  • Assisting in the graphic design and organization of the finalized booklet.
